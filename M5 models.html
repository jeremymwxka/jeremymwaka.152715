BMW M5 Models

The BMW M5 has a rich history spanning several generations, each offering unique features and performance characteristics. Here's a comprehensive list of BMW M5 models:

1. E28 BMW M5 (1985-1988)

The first-generation BMW M5, based on the E28 5 Series platform, was introduced in 1985. It featured a modified version of the M88/3 engine from the BMW M1 supercar, producing around 282 horsepower. The E28 M5 is celebrated for its understated design and pioneering the high-performance sedan segment.

Only available with a manual transmission, the E28 M5 emphasized driving engagement and precision. Its performance and handling capabilities were unmatched in its era, making it a sought-after classic among enthusiasts.

2. E34 BMW M5 (1989-1995)

The second-generation BMW M5, based on the E34 5 Series platform, debuted in 1989. It featured a more powerful 3.6-liter inline-six engine producing approximately 310 horsepower. The E34 M5 is praised for its balance of performance and luxury, setting new standards for executive sports sedans.

Notable advancements in the E34 M5 included a revised suspension system and electronic stability control, enhancing both comfort and handling characteristics. Its refined interior and timeless design continue to resonate with enthusiasts today.

3. E39 BMW M5 (1998-2003)

The third-generation BMW M5, based on the E39 5 Series platform, was introduced in 1998. It featured a 4.9-liter V8 engine producing 394 horsepower, paired with a six-speed manual transmission. The E39 M5 is revered for its exceptional handling and driving dynamics, earning accolades as one of the best sports sedans of its time.

With its powerful engine, precise steering, and well-balanced chassis, the E39 M5 offered a thrilling driving experience both on the track and the open road. Its timeless design and robust build quality have contributed to its enduring popularity among enthusiasts.

4. E60 BMW M5 (2005-2010)

The fourth-generation BMW M5, based on the E60 5 Series platform, debuted in 2005. It marked a departure from previous models with its V10 engine, delivering a staggering 500 horsepower. The E60 M5 introduced advanced technology features such as SMG transmission and active suspension, redefining the performance sedan segment.

Despite initial criticism of its controversial design and complex technology, the E60 M5 earned praise for its blistering acceleration and superb handling. It remains a landmark model in the M5 lineup, showcasing BMW's commitment to pushing the boundaries of performance and innovation.

5. F10 BMW M5 (2011-2016)

The fifth-generation BMW M5, based on the F10 5 Series platform, was launched in 2011. It featured a twin-turbocharged 4.4-liter V8 engine producing 560 horsepower, making it the most powerful M5 model at the time. The F10 M5 combined brute force with refined luxury, offering blistering acceleration and advanced driver assistance systems.

With its sophisticated chassis, adaptive suspension, and customizable driving modes, the F10 M5 delivered a comfortable and engaging driving experience in various conditions. Its spacious and tech-laden interior catered to both performance enthusiasts and luxury sedan buyers.

6. F90 BMW M5 (2018-present)

The current-generation BMW M5, based on the F90 5 Series platform, was introduced in 2018. It continues the tradition of performance excellence with a twin-turbocharged 4.4-liter V8 engine, now producing up to 617 horsepower in the Competition model. The F90 M5 sets new benchmarks for acceleration, handling, and technological innovation, cementing its status as a true super sedan.

Equipped with advanced features such as M xDrive all-wheel drive system and adaptive M suspension, the F90 M5 offers unparalleled performance and versatility. Its luxurious interior, cutting-edge infotainment system, and driver assistance technologies ensure a premium driving experience for enthusiasts and daily commuters alike.

Each generation of the BMW M5 represents a milestone in automotive engineering, pushing the boundaries of performance, luxury, and innovation.
